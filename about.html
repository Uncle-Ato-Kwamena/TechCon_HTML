History

TechCon has evolved from a small gathering of technology enthusiasts into one of the most prestigious tech conferences worldwide. Founded in 2010, TechCon has consistently brought together leading experts, innovators, and professionals to share insights and drive technological advancement. Over the years, we have hosted thousands of attendees, ranging from industry veterans to up-and-coming innovators, fostering a community of knowledge and collaboration.

Mission

Our mission at TechCon is to connect the brightest minds in technology, inspire innovation, and drive progress in the tech industry. We are committed to providing a platform where professionals can exchange ideas, learn from each other, and push the boundaries of what's possible in technology. Through collaboration and sharing knowledge, we aim to make a lasting impact on the future of technology

Mark Elliot Zuckerberg (born May 14, 1984) is an American businessman. He co-founded the social media service Facebook and its parent company Meta Platforms (formerly Facebook, Inc.), of which he is chairman, chief executive officer and controlling shareholder. Zuckerberg briefly attended Harvard University, where he launched Facebook in February 2004 with his roommates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz and Chris Hughes. Zuckerberg took the company public in May 2012 with majority shares. In 2008, at age 23, he became the world's youngest self-made billionaire. He has since used his funds to organize multiple donations, including the establishment of the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative.
